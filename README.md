# Incident Response Plan & Execution

Objective

Analyze a simulated ransomware incident impacting a Finance department and apply a structured incident response approach aligned with National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) guidelines. The goal is to assess the scope of compromise, implement containment and remediation strategies, and ensure business continuity through effective recovery and disaster recovery planning.

Methodology

1. Detection & Analysis: Identified affected assets and assessed scope of compromise across interconnected systems
2. Containment: Isolated infected systems and restricted access to prevent further spread
3. Eradication: Removed ransomware through system reimaging and elimination of malware
4. Recovery: Restored systems and data from backups and validated system integrity
5. Post-Incident Improvements: Implemented segmentation, least privilege access, and updated security policies.

Scenario Analysis

This scenario simulates a ransomware attack targeting a Finance department workstation within an enterprise environment. The initial compromise involved encryption of critical financial files required for business operations. Due to ineffective network segmentation, there is potential for lateral movement across systems, including shared drives and workstations. The incident prevents a risk to data availability and operational continuity, requiring rapid identification of affected assets and assessment of the overall scope of compromise to support incident response actions.
